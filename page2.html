What are the environmental consequences of computer device production?
Computer device production requires the harvesting of many minerals, such as gold, copper, silver, platinum, and much more. Extrating these elements deep underground can lead to deforestation, water contamination and habitat destruction. Producing computer components also requires large amounts of energy. Producing this much energy using unsustainable sources generates carbon emissions, which contribute to climate change.

How does computer use harm the environment?
Computers need energy to run. With the massive technological advancements allowing moore and more people around the world to have access to a computer, the amount of energy consumed is massive. Using unsustainable sources of energy, such as natural gas, will lead to climate change. Furthermore, e-waste generation pollutes surroundings by leaking harmful substances.

What are negative effects of computer device disposal?
Computer devices contain batteries. After being disposed of, these batteries will release harmful substances, such as lithium. Components of computers will also damage surrounding bodies of water and soil after being decomposed.
